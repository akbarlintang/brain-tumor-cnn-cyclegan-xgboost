About

This signifies the implementation of my undergraduate research thesis in Computer Science at UNNES. It involves the utilization of a combined algorithm comprising CNN, CycleGAN, and XGBoost to create a brain tumor classification model for brain MRI images. This system comprises of two main functions, a classifier and a generator.

The classifier model utilizes a combination of CNN, CycleGAN, and XGBoost algorithms to classify a brain MRI image. CycleGAN aids in data augmentation during model training, while CNN comprehends features within the input data and generates classification probabilities during training. Subsequently, XGBoost uses the probabilities from CNN to classify the input data. Currently, the classifier model achieved 97.37% accuracy on 38 testing data.

The generator model utilizes the CycleGAN algorithm to generate a new brain MRI image by transferring characteristics between tumor and non-tumor images. The model has been trained on 177 brain MRI images over 200 epochs.

The data used in this research was acquired from the Kaggle dataset named 'Brain MRI Images for Brain Tumor Detection'. The dataset comprises of 253 brain MRI images, categorized into two classes: 'yes' and 'no.'
